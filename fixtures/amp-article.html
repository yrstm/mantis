Coastal towns brace for autumn storms

Residents along the eastern coast spent the weekend stacking sandbags as forecasters warned of the strongest storm system to approach the region in over a decade.

Emergency services opened shelters in three counties and urged people in low-lying areas to move to higher ground before the tide peaks on Tuesday evening.

Power companies pre-positioned crews inland, saying they expect outages to last several days once winds exceed sixty miles per hour across the exposed headlands.
