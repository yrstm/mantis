The quiet economics of small ferries

Small passenger ferries rarely turn a profit on fares alone, yet hundreds of them keep running across bays and rivers that bridges never reached.

The math only works because the boats are treated as roads: subsidized as public infrastructure rather than judged as standalone businesses with their own balance sheets.
